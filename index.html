Abstract

Accurate text detection in images is a fundamental yet challenging task. Beyond simple localization, there's a crucial need for hierarchical text detection, which extracts structured groupings of words, lines, and paragraphs to support richer scene understanding and information retrieval. We propose FocusFormer, a robust text detection framework specifically designed for hierarchical text decoding across multiple granularities. FocusFormer features an adapted SAM backbone with a ViT-based encoder, followed by a point proposal network, TextPointNet, which generates text-specific point proposals. These proposals are processed by TextSegNet, a mask decoder explicitly designed to produce word-, line-, and paragraph-level masks simultaneously. Our approach also demonstrates strong performance on small and dense text detection tasks. Extensive results across text detection benchmarks show significant improvements in hierarchical detection: on the HierText test set, F1-scores improve by 16.6%, 4.4%, and 6.3% at the word, line, and paragraph levels, respectively; on TotalText, word-level F1 increases by 2.45%; and on CTW1500, line-level F1 improves by 2.27%. Additionally, our model demonstrates strong zero-shot capabilities, achieving F1-score gains of 8.32% on CTW1500, 8.93% on MSRA-TD500, and 10.29% on Occluded-RoadText when trained solely on HierText.

Modules

TextPointNet Module — The TextPointNet module leverages image features and n learnable queries to generate Text Point Proposals (TPPs) along with their corresponding class confidence scores. During training, Hungarian matching is employed to optimally align the predicted points with the ground-truth point proposals.

TextSegNet Module — The TextSegNet module takes image features and prompt embeddings as inputs to perform hierarchical text segmentation. Here, W, L, and P denote Word, Line, and Paragraph tokens, respectively.
